Luke 1, December 1, 2019

Posted on Sunday, 12/1/19

There are so many things in any chapter, but there are two things that stood out to me at today's reading of Luke’s account in chapter 1. I have always been and am still in joyous awe at the fact that Yochanan (John) was filled with Ruach HaKodesh (Holy Spirit) even from his mother’s womb! Verse 15. I mean, talk about maximizing your potential! To be filled with Holy Spirit even before you were born! Besides Jesus, you can’t get any more awesome than that! Oh! to have open eyes of who you are for your entire life, especially during your informative years would be astounding! Most of us simply struggle with adamic nature and then have plenty of time to nurture and develop that seed of sin inside of us. Then at some point in our life we have to “wrestle” with God trying to prove that we were right in our actions, until we finally realize and admit that maybe everything we’d been doing our entire life was actually wrong. Once we accept this reality, then we are able to surrender to pure love. I could go on forever, but this is to be a quick summary, so let me move onto the next event that is fascinating to me.

In verse 18 we see John’s father Zacharias question the angel about the real chance of having a son since he & his wife were old. The angel; in what seems to be an indignant response, tells Zacharias, I was sent from God to tell you the good news; in fact the answer to the prayers you have been asking God for years, and now you don’t believe me?! Because of your unbelief, you will be mute until the day of your son’s birth. Boom! Just like that Zacharias; who is a priest, can’t speak until the baby is born.

Then in verse 34, we see that the angel comes to Miryam (Mary) and tells her that she is going to conceive the Son of Ha’Elyon (The Most High - God) and she asks how this can be since she is still a virgin. The angel explains the supernatural process and Mary says, may it be done as you have said. She is left by the angel having been called blessed.

In both cases we have 2 people asking the messenger of God about the birth of their son. In the case of Zachariah; his question is, “How can I be sure?” In other words; I don’t believe you, what evidence can you give me that this is going to happen. Zacharias' question is a statement of doubt, the angel even says, “because you didn’t believe my words.” This doubt brings a time of silence for Zacharias. I’m sure this gave him a lot of time to think about why he doubted and most likely actually developed his faith stronger as he experienced the very real power of having the ability to speak stopped.

In Mary’s case, her question was not doubt, but an inquiry on how God would bring about this miracle. She was interested in how something would take place without man’s intervention in the process. She was fascinated by the reality that ANYTHING is possible with God. She showed a willingness to be a co-laborer with God by immediately answering the angel and saying "let it be so."

Two things come to mind; 1), prayer works, and when you pray for something within God’s will; you should expect it to come to pass. 2), instead of asking God to prove Himself to us by giving us proof that He will hold up His end of the bargain, we should shift our perceptions to ask God how we can prove ourselves to Him by being a part of what He is about to create. God always blesses those in His presence co-creating and bringing about His will on earth.

Luke 2, December 2, 2019

Posted on Monday, 12/2/19

The chapter starts out with what we like to call in my line of work, an unfunded government mandate. A legal requirement is placed on all people to go back to their family's homeland and enroll for a census. No monies are given to make the trip, no provisions made for livestock left behind, nothing is supplied to help in this forced journey demanded by Caesar Augustus. Ah, yes; there’s nothing that can tick me off faster than the abuse of power. Welcome to tyrant dictatorship whether ran by fear and intimidation or by sleight-of-hand politics and deception, it is all a betrayal of relationship and community.

But the goodness of God is found everywhere, even in the midst of this particular hardship. In verse 8 we see that there are shepherds in the same country guarding their flocks. Suddenly, an angel shows up in the middle of the night to a bunch of lowly hard-working shepherds who had a reputation for smelling bad due to the sweat of their labor, the smell of their livestock, and living in the dirt with the sheep kind of lifestyle. They were often out on their own, and easily forgotten as they were an out-of-sight, out-of-mind kind of a vagabond with very little respect from society. But that was no deterrent for God’s angel and he brings the good news of great joy that will be for ALL people, even social outcasts as themselves! The angel goes on to tell them where they can see the newborn Savior of the world for themselves. Then in verse 13; hundreds, maybe even thousands of angels start having a worship service in the night sky right in front of the shepherds! What!?! I mean come on! How many of you have ever got to be in a worship service with angels!??? The angels didn’t perform for important dignitaries like kings and queens, but put on a show for the poor, the humble, the forgotten, and the rejects of society. Not only did they sing and praise in front of what the world would call the lowliest of humans but invited them into this grand cosmic celebration of the best event in all of earth’s history!!!

On the one hand; Mary and Joseph are suffering an unreasonable hardship traveling miles on a donkey, fully swollen and pregnant. But on the other hand, this bummer trip put them in the right place (or country as the scripture says) for the shepherds to experience the most exhilarating moment in their natural lives. Mary was already blessed, and in her moment of pain, her hardship literally became a blessing for those in their own hardship of life.

In a world where the powerful create a society that defines who is in and who is out; in a man created culture where privilege and authority stays with the rich and the powerful at the cost of the poor, God reminds us that our riches and power mean nothing to the Creator of the universe and that He is a respecter of no man. And just in case you missed it; God drives the point home by sending the Son of God to earth to be born in an animals feeding trough, in an even lower caste than the shepherds. Jesus comes to level the playing field for everyone. He comes to show the world the truth which is no matter who you are, what you’ve done, how great or how poor you are, you are just as important and valuable as anyone else. Male or female, athletic or pathetic, bright or a little light; all of heaven gave everything to love you and Jesus died for you specifically. This is truly good news for all of us!

Luke 3, December 3, 2019

Posted on Tuesday, 12/3/19

There is a lot of meat in this chapter & I had to do a little research to try to get a grasp of some concepts here. Allow me to try to summarize and familiarize you with a few things that were new to me. If I were to ask you, what are the 3 top things people of this world want, what would you come up with? Money, power, and sex perhaps? One would only have to watch 30 seconds of TV to see that obvious reality right? So let’s take a look at this chapter because I think you may see some correlations.

ROMAN HIERARCHY (in order of rank)

  1. EMPEROR: “King” of all of Rome, richest most powerful man in the world.
  2. TETRARCH: the governor of one of four divisions of a country or province in the Roman empire.
  3. LEGATUS AUGUSTI PROPARETORE: the military governor of a province of the empire, head of the judicial system. The highest rank an officer could achieve. Top dog of the military.
  4. LEGATUS LEGIONIS: Commander of a legion (5000 men divided into 10 cohorts). Usually came from the wealthy or privileged.
  5. TRIBUNUS LATICLAVIUS: 2nd in command of a legion. Also reserved typically for aristocrats of Rome.
  6. PRAEFECTUS CASTRORUM: Third in command of a legion.
  7. EQUESTRIAN PRAEFECTI: Commander of a horse auxillary in the legion. Horse or equestrian commanders were more prestigious than infantry commanders.
  8. TRIBUNE AGUSTICLAVII: Staff officer, below Equestrian Praefecti but above Infantry Praefecti. 5 in each legion.
  9. INFANTRY PRAEFECTI: Commander of an infantry cohort.
  10. CENTURION: Commander of a century (about 80 men) in a legion. The highest rank an ordinary/common man could expect to achieve.

Ok, now that you have a flavor for that, let’s take a gander at a map to help organize our geographical awareness.

Cool, now let’s add a few names from scripture to paint the background for this chapter and to get a sense of who’s who in the zoo.

  1. Tiberius: Emperor of Rome.
  2. Herod: Tetrarch of Galilee.
  3. Philip (Pilipos): Herod’s brother, Tetrarch of Ituraea & Trachonitis
  4. Lysanias: Tetrarch of Abilene
  5. Pontius Pilate: Military governor of Judea. As head of the judicial system, he had the power to execute capital punishment (as in Jesus execution), as well as appoint the high priests. Some ancient test suggest that Pontius Pilate and Caephaes were friends. Now that sheds some new light on the trial of Jesus, or lack of.
  6. Annas & Caiaphas: Jewish high priesthood.

    7. Ok, so we got the backdrop from verses 1 - 3, now let’s jump into the rest of the story.

    V3 John comes into ALL the region around the Jordan. Goes from before the Sea of Galilee to the Dead sea. The Jordan runs right through the middle of all the Roman Empire. Repentance is preached to the ENTIRE Roman Empire.

    V5 every valley will be filled – that which was empty will be made full (I can’t help but think of our souls and being empty of righteousness, will now be made full of God’s righteousness when we repent or our sin). Every mountain/hill will be brought low – giants obstacles will be crushed (Jesus will take all the hard challenges in our life and make them bow so that nothing will stand in the way of God’s children to reach our destiny in Christ). The crooked will become straight – (Jesus will make the path to God it’s shortest and easiest to for us to maneuver.) rough ways smooth (reiteration of Jesus making everything easy for us if we will repent or turn away from our own direction and follow Him.)

    V6 As a result of what Jesus will do, all flesh will see God’s Salvation.

    V 7-10 John confirms to the Roman empire that they are poisonous venomous snakes - the same deceitful creature in the garden of Eve that caused all of humanity to fall. He warns them that they can not deceive their way out of the coming wrath and that they must show true fruits of repentance. Keep in mind he is speaking to the Roman empire who has conquered the known world, is used to having power and riches as a nation and accustomed to abusing that power anyway they wish.

    V11 John addresses each social caste within the Roman empire and starts with the lowest or general population. He tells those who have extra, give to those who need. Sounds a lot like the 2nd commandment of love thy neighbor as you love yourself, doesn’t it?

    V12 He addresses the wealthy and tells the tax collectors to only collect what they are supposed to. Isn’t there a commandment that says, thou shall not steal?

    V14 He addresses those with power and tells them not to violently steal, or falsely accuse anyone, and to be happy with their salary. Hmm...thou shall not covet sounds like solid advice.

    V19 & 20 we see that Herod, a powerful tetrarch with both money and power had yet a third thing which he thought was a privilege that he deserved and had actually succumbed to lust and had his brother’s wife. He chose his lust (a thing which can never be satisfied and leads to wanting more and more outrageous cravings than the last) over God’s salvation and imprisoned John. This is a tragic example of some men wanting darkness over light. This is also an example of man thinking they have the power to stop truth or the authority to escape God’s wrath by imprisoning those who bring the gospel.

    V23 The supernatural manifests in the flesh realm again. Not only was Jesus the living, earthly, human manifestation of God standing right in the middle of the world for all to see, but God again drives His point home by sending the Holy Spirit in the likeness of a form of a dove to sit upon Jesus. And just to make it even clearer for anyone still wondering what’s going on, God SPEAKS from heaven and says, This is my Son in whom I am well pleased!!! Wowweeee! “What must we do?” the people ask. If you are asking what should you do to please God and God says I am pleased in my beloved son, may I offer that maybe you should do what Jesus does????

    V23 We start off this chapter with the hierarchy of power and prestige of the Roman Empire, but then Dr. Luke finishes up with the lineage of Jesus. There is a whole other study here to go through all the relationship of the lineage of Jesus, but that will have to be it’s very own research page.

    Jesus - the son (as supposed - because He was actually not born of the seed of Joseph but from the Holy Spirit) of Joseph. Another spiritual to flesh manifestation. This is hard for us to wrap our heads around but remember anything IS POSSIBLE with God, especially those things that are NOT POSSIBLE with man. Still God communicates in a way that we can understand and this is noted because it is how the Jewish understood family lineage - through the male. The rest of the chapter goes all the way through the human lineage and finishes with Adam - the son of God (the very first spirit to human flesh manifestation). Aren’t you glad to be a son or daughter of God?

    Luke 4, December 4, 2019

    Posted on Wednessday, 12/4/19

    3”And the devil said unto him, If thou be the Son of God, command this stone that it be made bread. 4And Jesus answered him, saying, It is written, That man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word of God.” This passage is interesting because of the word “WORD.” In the book Pneumatology, The Ecumenical, International, and Contextual Perspective by Velli-Matti Karkkainen, it discusses a theologian named Maximus the Confessor, possibly a Syrian Monk in the 5th or 6th century in the Eastern Christian Church who makes the following statement: “The Holy Spirit is not absent from any created being, especially not from one which in any way participates in intelligence...The Holy Spirit is present unconditionally in all things, in that He embraces all things, provides for all, and vivifies the natural seeds within them...He embraces in unity the spiritual knowledge of all created things, providentially permeating all things with His power, and vivifying their inner essence in accordance with their nature.” Simply put, the Spirit of God has made everything that exists as a living being. It occurred to me that my own Native American culture and religion believed that all things are alive, since everything can consume on level. Rocks can actually absorb water for example. Then I think of John 1:1 In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. And then again in John 1:14 The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us. We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth. I also think of the scripture that says that everything that has breath will return back to God. Ecclesiates 12:7 then the dust will return to the earth as it was, and the spirit will return to God who gave it. The book went on to say, that some schools of thought offer that the Holy Spirit can possess words. If the Spirit of God can make donkeys speak and the rocks cry out praise, then what is so hard to believe that the Holy Spirit can possess words too? If the Word became flesh and dwelt among us on the earth as Jesus, why can’t every word have Holy Spirit power and authority? It suddenly occurred to me that our prayers can have the Holy Spirit possess them. It suddenly made sense to me why as a child of God, I have the heritage to speak blessings or cursing, life or death and that when I command an illness or demon to leave by the authority of the name (Word) of Jesus that it has to obey! I’ve seen many a miracle in my lifetime already. The Lord has blessed by using me as a vessel to bring a gift of healing to someone. Even with all that, I couldn’t really understand why and kept looking for a pattern to repeat or some action that I needed to do in order to have miracles, signs, and wonders following me. I knew that it was never I that did anything, but the Holy Spirit. Yet, my logical mind was always in overdrive trying to analyze everything. When Jesus said that man doesn’t live by bread alone, but by every Word of God, He really meant that it is God that gives life to man, and not the eating or nurturing of the physical body as we assume. Everything that ever came into existence is because God spoke it into being and we live each day by God’s daily bread and Word that He speaks every moment to sustain our life. Can the concept of devotion and daily communion and relationship with God suddenly seem more urgent to you? It should, because without it, life in your mortal body will not continue and the longer it is without the constant nourishment of the Word of God, it deteriorates more and more every day. “For the wages of sin (opposite direction or missing the mark of God’s will) is death. Romans 6:23. Jesus says, “...I (the WORD) came that they may have life and have it abundantly.” John 10:10.

    In verse 18 we see what Jesus is tasked to do with WORDS. To declare hope to the discouraged, to release those chained in bondage, to heal the blind, to encourage the broken-hearted, to proclaim the acceptable year of the Lord, the return of the King.

    Starting in verse 31 we read accounts of Jesus casting out demons. In verse 38 we read accounts of Jesus healing one of the disciples mother-in-laws (healing for the house of Israel or what we would call today our church). Then in verse 40 we see Jesus heals everything and everyone including those not within the house of Israel (the gentiles or what we would today call those outside our congregations).

    What great news for everyone! There is healing for all of us! And this good, gracious, life-giving, redemptive, message is known as the gospel. And this is how Jesus finishes the fourth chapter of Luke, he reminds everyone that He must preach the GOOD NEWS. Jesus must announce His Holy Spirit WORD to the entire world!!!

    Luke 5, December 5, 2019

    Posted on Wednessday, 12/5/19

    In the first 11 verses, Jesus is swarmed by people wanting to hear the WORD of God, but Jesus notices 2 boats with fishermen nearby working. Jesus goes to Shim’on (Simon’s) boat and asks to be taken out a little ways from the shore and then starts teaching the crowd from there. So cool that Jesus has this entire flock following Him around groping for His attention, but Jesus notices a few men who are not looking for His attention, who are busy trying to make ends meet by working all night. Rather than turning an indignant shoulder to them, He immediately engages them into His mission. Then after he feeds the crowds & by default the fishermen; God’s WORD, Jesus then helps the fishermen finish what they were working so hard to accomplish. I love the fact that Jesus isn’t a user. He doesn’t commandeer the fishermen’s boats, take-up their already over-booked time, then leave them further behind in their duties, but blesses them with a miracle of catching more fish in a moment than they were able to do on their own, even after working all night long.

    In verse 5, Peter recognizes and respects Jesus as a Rabbi, but doesn’t believe the Rabbi’s WORD initially and protests telling Jesus that they have already tried everything all night long and still have not been able to bring in a catch. But out of cultural respect for Rabbis, he does what Jesus directs him to do. Jesus, who is a carpenter by trade, not a fisherman proves to Peter, James, and John that their wisdom and experience in fishing their whole lives is not as able as the power & authority of the WORD of God.

    In verse 10; the fishing crew is astonished at the massive catch of fish they recieve by obeying Jesus and suddenly understand their sinful unbelief. All three fall to their knees and declare their shame by telling Jesus to leave from their unholy presence as they are sinners. What a wonderful moment of fear and reverence must have come over the fisherman for them to bow at Jesus feet. No doubt they were probably questioning their earthly wisdom and competence when they couldn’t bring in a haul for the day, but what they perceive as lack of earthly wisdom, God replaces in the moment with heaven’s wisdom. For “The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom, and knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.” Proverbs 9:10. Notice Jesus doesn’t hurl an insulting, “I told you so!” at them but rather calms their fears and tells them not to be afraid. But it gets even better than that! After Jesus calms their fears, he then employs them in spreading the Good News and informs them that they will now be a fisher of men!

    What popped out at me today was Verse 16, where Jesus made a habit of pulling away from the crowds so that He could pray. He found the fishermen working overtime trying to make ends meet and not taking the time for spiritual disciplines like praying. Jesus has many many people who want His healing touch. Jesus has more work than any man could possibly accomplish on his own, yet Jesus makes taking time to do quality prayer time a priority. He doesn’t let the overwhelming amount of tasks or the pressing cry of the crowds steal His time with the Father away. I think this is a very important lesson that the LORD is showing us here. Ministering to people can be heart-breaking. Because many Christians love people and have such big hearts, it can be difficult to say no to those begging for our attention, but Jesus the Minister or all ministers, reminds us that we can not live without the WORD of God. We must make time away with God the priority over everything else, even ministry.

    Luke 6, December 6, 2019

    Posted on Thursday, 12/6/19

    Verse 23 takes the cake for today’s reading. Luke 6:23 “Rejoice and leap for joy on that day! Behold, your reward will be great in heaven. For their ancestors treated the prophets in the same way.” This passage is literally in the middle of the chapter. With 49 verses; it is the end of the first half of the chapter as 24 is the beginning of the second half to be exact. The verses immediately before it and immediately following it make a nice little chiasm. Example of what I’m talking about:

    ORIGIN VERSE FUTURE VERSE
    20 Blessed are you who are poor, for the kingdom of God is yours. 24 But woe to you who are rich, for you have received your consolation.
    21 Blessed are you who are now hungry, for you will be satisfied. 25 But woe to you who are filled now, for you will be hungry.
    22 Blessed are you when people hate you, (and speak ill of you)... 26 Woe to you when all speak well of you,

    Then the end of 26 ties it back into the reason to rejoice and dance when you’re persecuted by turning the tables and showing what will happen to those who tell lies. Verse 26b…”for their ancestors treated the false prophets in this way.”

    There’s a lot of discussion about Christian ethics in this chapter. A lot of stories about grace vs. the law. And right in the middle of all of it, Jesus exhorts us to stay strong, to stay true, and in fact to know that even in the face of poverty, hunger, and defamation; know that in the end; we win! And that winning will be worth all the suffering we may experience now. So certain of victory is Jesus that He backs it up with proof. He shows us history repeats itself by reminding us that the prophets of God were treated poorly by the same ancestors of those hurting us now. In other words, satan’s offspring aren’t doing anything new to God’s children. But, rest assured; as proof through repeated history again and again, we see that those who are living in opulence now through corruption, will be brought into poverty and hunger later. And those who slander us now will receive their own punishments and even death as a false prophet later just like it was of old.

    Luke 7, December 7, 2019

    Posted on Thursday, 12/7/19

    47 Because of this, I tell you that her sins — which are many! — have been forgiven, because she loved much. But someone who has been forgiven only a little loves only a little.” 48 Then he said to her, “Your sins have been forgiven.”

    Some of us are very passionate. We think deeply and feel intensely. We are called emotional and often thought of inferior somehow because we are so easily overwhelmed by our emotions and therefore it is assumed that we cannot make sound logical decisions. That assumption is just simply inaccurate. This is often why children and women are considered the weaker. While it may take more time to process emotions, emotional/passionate people absolutely can make sound logical decisions. In fact our ability to consider the cost of our decision is enhanced because we are able to take into account not only the material risk and value of the things involved in the decision but we are able to ascertain and recognize the value of the human condition and factor it into the cost. For example, in business, all decisions are based upon profit and the ability to make money. It is the most powerful motivation in the industry. But if you were to ask any terminally ill patient what they want more than anything, you will find that money is not even in the top 5 requests. What they have come to understand is that time is the most important thing and it is not so that they can make more money but so they could have loved more. They would give up all the money in the world if they could have more time to spend holding their grandchildren, more time to restore their relationships with their estranged children, more time to see the wonders and glories of God’s creation, more time to appreciate their spouses, more time to experience the gift of love, peace, joy, and explore the wonders of the emotions God created us with.

    Those who would lord over us with intellectual prowess often are involved in authoritarian positions. They tend to keep us oppressed by creating rules to develop a society that says emotions are forbidden in the workplace or other communal properties and are to be considered highly unprofessional. While I agree that we must exercise control over emotions that would lead to harming ourselves or others, we should respect the rights and presence of others, and the sensual emotions of lust must be restrained; those who think they are superior because of their ability to turn off their emotions would have the passionate believe that they are more akin to animals than humans. This is just another form of oppression through identity abuse.

    In verses 39-43, Jesus perceives the Pharisees critical thought about allowing a sinner woman to wash His feet. Jesus tells him a story about money, something he can understand. He asks if 2 people owed someone money, one of them owing 10 times more than the other and both their debts were dropped, who would love the forgiver more? Of course the one owing the most money would love or be more grateful. Then Jesus compares the woman to the same story. He says, this woman who you look at with disgust and whose sins are many are forgiven because she loves much. Then Jesus tells the woman your trust (in Me), your faith has saved you, go in peace. In other words, she was forgiven. Scripture doesn’t record that she asked for forgiveness, but Jesus knows the intent of the heart. The woman found out that Jesus was eating at Simons the Pharisees house. She must have known who Jesus was. She weeps at His feet and washes them with her tears and her hair. She comes undone in front of the Son of God. This is a sign of humbleness, my guess is that it was an acknowledgement of the contrast of her sinful nature to the holiness of God’s nature, just like when Peter and the fisherman bowed before Jesus when they realized they were sinners in the presence of the Holy God.

    Then Jesus drops the bomb by explaining to the Pharisee, that she has been forgiven much which has caused her to love much, passionately. The woman allowed her intense emotions to communicate her remorse for her sin and plead the forgiveness of Jesus. When Jesus blesses her, gives her forgiveness, she leaves. Yet the Pharisee who has followed all the rules, reasons that he safe because he can use his intelligence to quote the law and is smart enough to use it masterfully as his defense. But those who have not been forgiven much do not love much, they don’t feel the need to be grateful.

    While knowledge and intelligence are definitely gifts of God also, and I am glad for those who excel at it, it is not a gift more superior than others. Just like being passionate does not make someone inferior in any way either. Jesus uses all gifts of both intelligence and emotion or passion. In the time of temptation in the wilderness He used His intelligence to quote law and scripture that the devil tried to twist. But He endured the cross for the JOY (passion/emotion) set before Him. In the end, LOVE is always the final battle.
